FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What are GCMS notes? +
GCMS (Global Case Management System) notes are internal records that IRCC immigration officers create while processing your immigration application. They contain detailed information about your case history, officer comments, and decision-making process.
Who can request GCMS notes? +
Any individual who has or had an immigration application in Canada can request their own GCMS notes. This includes PR applicants, temporary residents, refugee claimants, and Canadian citizens. You can also request notes for a family member with their written consent.
How long does it take to receive GCMS notes? +
IRCC is legally required to respond to ATIP requests within 30 days (with possible extensions). In practice, processing typically takes 30–90 days. Our Express plan prioritizes your submission and provides faster initiation.
Is my personal information safe? +
Absolutely. We take privacy very seriously. All information is encrypted, stored securely, and used only to process your ATIP request. We comply with Canada's Privacy Act and PIPEDA regulations.
What information do I need to provide? +
You'll need to provide your full legal name, date of birth, UCI number (if available), email address, and a copy of your government-issued ID. This information is required by IRCC to process your request.
Do you offer refunds? +
Yes. If we are unable to obtain your GCMS notes for any reason, we offer a full refund. Our money-back guarantee ensures you're protected. Please review our refund policy for complete details.
